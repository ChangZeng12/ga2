Core Mechanics

Geometry Arena 2 is a free-form arena-shooter Roguelite game, dedicated to creating an exciting battle experience.

Freely customize weapons, bodies, modules, upgrades and challenges to create your own unique gaming experience!

GA1

Geometry Arena(GA) is a the is a pop-up shooter that combines RPG and Roguelite elements. The game presents rich gameplay with simple geometric painting style and rustic light effects, and is thus jokingly called “the light pollution game” by players. Variety, freedom, and endlessness are the core keywords of Geometry Arena.

UX Research Findings

While GA1 players enjoyed the game, they also reported many problems with the UI in the game. The amount of text is too large resulting in a lack of readability, and the current interface design has become very crowded after the game has been updated several times.

Design Concepts

Minimalism

Unlike the graphics of GA1, GA2 will use a new cartoony minimalist theme

Expandability

GA2's UI will be very expandable to ensure that it can accommodate future updates to the game

Readability

GA2's text will be color-coded to ensure that players get the information they want quickly.

Wireframes

We spent a lot of time drawing wireframes before the formal design, which gave us a chance to make sense of the interaction logic of the interfaces at a much lower cost. At this stage, we also determined the distribution of information on each page.

Lo-fi prototype

After wireframe is complete, a quick build lofi interface can help programmers quickly build MVPs in the game engine.

This allows us to get hands-on experience on the ground to see if the interaction design is really interesting, especially if the switching between the game levels and the menu interface is smooth.

Design System

Hi-fi prototype

Hi-Fi prototype started once the team finalized the final flowchart and design system.

The final UI design utilizes the high contrast flat geometric art style described in the design system. Smooth interactive animations were also heavily utilized in the game, and the speed curves of the various animations were carefully tuned to create a dynamic and lovely overall look and feel.

Conclusions

GA2 is still in the Early Access phase right now, which means the game is still expanding its gameplay.

The game's UI has managed to be compatible with the content updates from launch to now. The possibility of gameplay going beyond the current design framework may arise in the future, and I will update here if there are any design changes.
